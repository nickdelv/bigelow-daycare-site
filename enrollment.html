Enrollment

Tuition, schedules, and how to apply.

Bigelow is currently accepting applications for enrollment. The best first step is to schedule a tour — we'll walk you through everything from there.

How to Enroll

Three steps to get started.

01
Schedule a tour

The best way to get a feel for Bigelow is to visit in person. Tours are at 9am, last 30–45 minutes, and children are welcome.

Request a tour →

02
Submit an application

After your tour, we'll share an application and walk you through next steps. There is a $50 application fee, waived for families with state child care vouchers.

03
Sign your contract

Enrollment is confirmed with a signed 12-month contract and a one-month tuition deposit. The deposit is applied toward your August tuition the following year.

Bigelow is open Monday through Friday, 8:30am–5:30pm. The core program day runs 8:30am–3:30pm. Extended day care until 5:30pm is available as an add-on.

Bigelow is open 49 weeks a year. We close for standard holidays, the last week of August, the week between Christmas and New Year's, and April vacation week. We also close for two professional development days per year.

As a cooperative, families are active members of the school community. This means:

Each family participates in a two-hour classroom work shift twice a month. Families also attend community and classroom meetings (roughly every other month), and join one parent committee — hiring, finance, web and technology, physical space, and others. One clean-up day each fall and spring is also expected.

Single-parent families are exempt from committee service, and any hardship that prevents participation will be accommodated.

We understand that work schedules don't always make on-site participation possible. As a community working together to meet the needs of both children and families, Bigelow is committed to finding solutions that work for everyone.

Families who are unable to meet the classroom work shift expectation are encouraged to reach out to the director — there are other ways to get involved, and Director Dawn Lorino is happy to identify options that fit your situation.

Parents designate authorized caregivers by signing an authorization form and introducing them to their child's teachers. All caregivers sign in and out every day.

Families provide diapers and lunch for their child. Bigelow provides wipes, two snacks daily, and organic milk at lunch in accordance with USDA guidelines.
